ESEENTIAL TIPS TO REDUCE YOUR RISK FROM TERRORISM WHILE TRAVELLING



The definition of terrorism is, in the broadest sense, the unlawful use of violence and intimidation, especially against civilians, in the pursuit of political, religious or ideological aims.

International terrorism remains a serious threat to Western nationals living or travelling overseas. Terrorist attacks can occur anywhere in the world, with little or no warning. There is considered to be a heightened threat of terrorist attack globally against Western nationals from groups or individuals motivated by conflicts in Iraq and Syria as well as Daesh the Islamist terrorist group, who have been responsible for many attacks throughout Europe.

While the probability of encountering a terrorist incident while travelling is low, taking proactive measures to reduce your risk from terrorism is essential for ensuring a safe and enjoyable journey.

TERRORIST TARGETS

Terrorists often look for targets that aren’t well-protected public places, and where westerners can be found to gain maximum media impact. Places that have been targeted in the past include bars, restaurants, concerts, shops, places of worship, tourist sites and transport networks. Significant dates, anniversaries, public holidays, religious festivals and political events have also been targeted.

Terrorist groups continue to use social media to inspire or direct so-called ‘lone wolf’ attacks which are difficult to predict and disrupt and could take place in almost any country.

In recent years in cities such as London, Barcelona and Nice lone-wolf attackers have used lorries and hire vans to carry out their atrocities. These types of attacks are impossible to predict and impossible to prevent.

If you are travelling in unfamiliar surroundings always keep your eyes and ears open and be aware of your surroundings and any exit routes which will allow you to move away from the danger as quickly as possible.

PEACE OF MIND: TRAVELING AMID THE FEAR OF TERRORISM

It’s natural to feel unnerved and anxious ahead of international travel. Terrorist actions and threats affect nearly every corner of the globe and make headlines in world news.

It's important to keep in mind that, proportionally, the likelihood of being involved in a terrorist incident is very small. However, there are a few simple steps that concerned travelers can take to further lower their risk. The most significant risks facing travelers are accidents—specifically road traffic accidents—and becoming ill. We travel for many reasons—to explore and experience another culture, to study, to research, to conduct business, and to build relationships. Those reasons should remain a focus.

Two of our responsibilities in GSS are to help Harvard travelers stay safe abroad through proper preparation and to support them in times of need following any type of critical incident, whether it's an accident, an illness, a natural disaster, or an attack. Our international safety & security team closely monitors events around the globe via private, commercial, and government sources. We use this information to track patterns, advise travelers, and respond swiftly when incidents occur.

AVIATION AND AIRPORT SECURITY

The risk to aviation including direct attacks at airports is clear from recent terrorist attacks. No country can give an absolute guarantee of security standards at its airports, but some airports may be less able to protect against terrorist attacks than others.

Once you have arrived at the international airport don’t hang around the open public spaces, check your luggage with the airline as soon as you can and move straight through customs and security and into the airside part of the airport, this minimises your risk being caught in any potential attack.

Recently a number of countries have introduced several new security measures for flights departing from Turkey, Egypt, Tunisia, Jordan, Saudi Arabia and Lebanon. Under the new measures, phones, laptops and tablets won’t be allowed in the cabin these devices must be placed in hold-stowed luggage.

REDUCE YOUR RISK FROM TERRORISM

First and foremost when travelling abroad, just like home, you are responsible for your own personal safety. You should always be aware of your surroundings, and report any concerns to the local security authorities. As a general rule, you should follow the advice of competent local authorities for the country you’re in. Here are some essential tips to help you reduce the risk of terrorism while travelling abroad:

Stay Informed

Before embarking on your journey, research the current political and security situation of your destination country. Stay updated with travel advisories issued by your government and international organisations. Understanding potential risks will enable you to make informed decisions and adapt your plans accordingly.

Blend in with your surroundings

While it’s natural to stand out as a foreigner in a new country, try to blend in with the local culture and customs as much as possible. Avoid drawing unnecessary attention to yourself by dressing modestly and respecting local traditions. Being inconspicuous can help reduce the likelihood of becoming a target for terrorists.

Be aware of your surroundings

Vigilance is key to staying safe while travelling. Pay attention to your surroundings, especially in crowded places such as airports, train stations, tourist attractions, and public events. Remain observant of any suspicious activities or individuals and trust your instincts if something feels off.

Avoid high-risk areas

Exercise caution when visiting high-risk areas such as border regions, military installations, government buildings, and known terrorist targets. Be mindful of travel restrictions and security advisories issued for specific regions or landmarks, and adhere to them accordingly.

Trust local authorities

In the event of a terrorist incident or security threat, follow the instructions of local authorities and emergency responders. Familiarise yourself with emergency procedures and evacuation routes at your accommodation and tourist sites, and act calmly and swiftly if the need arises.

While it’s impossible to eliminate all risks while travelling abroad, taking proactive measures to reduce your vulnerability to terrorism can significantly enhance your safety and peace of mind.

By staying informed, exercising caution, and adopting a vigilant mindset, you can minimise the likelihood of encountering security threats and focus on enjoying your travels to the fullest.

WHAT TRAVELERS SHOULD KNOW:

  • We'll continue to monitor the situation around the world.
  • Attacks in New Zealand, London, Istanbul, Brussels, Paris, Mali, Jakarta, Burkina Faso, and the United States over the past several years demonstrate that serious incidents can occur anywhere.
  • The potential for future incidents exists.
  • The likelihood of being involved in a terrorist incident is very, very small.

WHAT TRAVELERS SHOULD DO:

  • Register your trip with International SOS and update it if plans change.
  • Monitor trusted news sources and sign up for International SOS alerts via email or the Assistance App.
  • Register your travel with your embassy.
  • Carry proper identification at all times.
  • Have a communications plan that includes regularly scheduled check-ins and what to do and whom to contact in an emergency.
  • Allow extra time to pass through security.
  • Remain calm, flexible, and aware of your surroundings.
  • Familiarize yourself with your neighborhood and place of work or study.
  • Practice visualization exercises by mentally walking through "What if?" scenarios to plan how you would react. This preparation improves reaction time and mitigates shock and stress during a real emergency.
  • Cooperate with local law enforcement and heed their advice.
  • Avoid large protests and marches, even if they're peaceful.
